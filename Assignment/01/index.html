He gained international fame with his debut novel, Things Fall Apart (1958), which explores the impact of colonialism on Igbo society. Achebe's works, including Arrow of God, No Longer at Ease, and Anthills of the Savannah, address themes of tradition, identity, and power struggles in post-colonial Africa. A strong advocate for African storytelling, Achebe challenged Western narratives about Africa. He also worked as an editor, academic, and political activist. His literary contributions earned him numerous awards, including the Man Booker International Prize in 2007.
